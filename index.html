01 — D28 ROAS

D28 now sees more of your players

D7 is great at catching the players who pay fast. But for a lot of games, more than half of a player’s value shows up after the first week — and D7 just can’t see it. So we taught Vector to optimize on a 28-day horizon. You set a country-level ROAS target; Vector predicts each user’s 28-day value and bids accordingly.

Now live: D28 IAP ROAS — for IAP-heavy games with longer payback curves.

Open beta (worldwide): D28 Ad Revenue ROAS and D28 Hybrid ROAS — long-horizon optimization for ad-funded and mixed-monetization games.

What the beta is showing
Share of qualifying beta games that beat their paired D7 campaign (same game ID & geo)

One thing to remember: D28 runs alongside D7, not instead of it. Launch D7 first, let it pass Learning, then spin up D28 in the same geo so it skips Learning and optimizes from day one.

02 — ROAS Onboarding

We trimmed the friction. You can launch ROAS campaigns from day one (no detour through CPI), add new countries to existing campaigns more easily, and the dashboard now flags data-readiness or measurement issues before they slow you down. Learning vs. Live states are clearer too.

Set your Target Cost Per Event, and Unity Vector dynamically optimizes bids toward users most likely to complete that event — same engine, different signal.

05 — SDK 4.18+

A faster, steadier SDK

It’s easy to treat an SDK update as routine maintenance. This one isn’t. The latest Unity Ads SDK (4.18+) starts faster, delivers more reliably, and feeds cleaner runtime signals into optimization — which means less drop-off between load and show, and fewer opportunities lost before the auction even begins.

In our pre/post analyses: up to 59% faster init, stronger load-to-show (+32% Android, +66% iOS), and roughly ~20% more impressions and ~19% more revenue per DAU.

Internal results, not guaranteed outcomes — they vary with implementation, traffic mix, and mediation setup.

06 — Data Activation

Fewer blind spots, smarter optimization

Optimization is only as good as what it can see. Most studios aren’t short on data — it’s just scattered across attribution, behavior tools, and revenue systems. We’ve made it easier to connect those signals so Vector works with the full picture:

Google Analytics linking
A quick, no-code, 3-step link for your GA/Firebase behavior signals. Attribution tells you how players arrived; this tells you what they actually do.
MMP data quality
Cleaner attribution windows, organic data, and revenue-event mapping. Bad inputs mean bad optimization, so this matters more than it might look.
Android license key & IAP signals
Stronger Android revenue validation and cleaner purchase signals.
Developer Data Framework (DDF)
The permission layer that unlocks approved data for optimization — you stay in control of how your data is used.

And on the privacy side: Unity recently joined IAB Europe’s Transparency & Consent Framework — a recognized industry standard for collecting and respecting user consent across European markets. As you connect more signals, they’re handled to that standard.
